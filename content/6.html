Financial News of the Past Week



The past week was full of news about the financial markets and finance. Last week, the Federal Reserve (FED) announced a new round of quantitative easing, which is expected to help stimulate the economy and provide a boost to the markets. This was welcomed by investors, as it provided more certainty to the market.

In addition, the US Treasury Department released its first-quarter GDP report, which showed that the economy grew at an annualized rate of 4.2%, the highest growth since the fourth quarter of 2018. This was a good sign for the US economy and the markets, showing that the economy is continuing to improve.

In Europe, the EU Commission released its latest economic outlook, which indicated that the region is still facing some economic headwinds, but that overall it is performing better than expected. This was welcome news to investors, as it gave more confidence to the markets.

Finally, the stock markets around the world were mostly up last week, as investors were optimistic about the outlook for the global economy. With the US economy continuing to grow and the markets continuing to remain positive, investors are expecting the global economy to continue to improve in the coming months.
